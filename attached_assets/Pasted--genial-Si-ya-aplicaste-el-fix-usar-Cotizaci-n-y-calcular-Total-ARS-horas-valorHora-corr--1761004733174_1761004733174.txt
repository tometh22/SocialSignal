3) Validá detalle por persona (debe calzar con tu captura)
$PSQL <<'SQL'
SELECT
  dp.name                                     AS persona,
  ROUND(flm.target_hours::numeric,2)          AS horas_obj,
  ROUND(flm.asana_hours::numeric,2)           AS horas_asana,
  ROUND(flm.billing_hours::numeric,2)         AS horas_fact,
  ROUND(flm.hourly_rate_ars::numeric,2)       AS valor_hora_ars,
  ROUND((flm.billing_hours*flm.hourly_rate_ars)::numeric,2) AS total_ars_calc,
  ROUND(flm.cost_ars::numeric,2)              AS total_ars_db,
  ROUND(flm.fx::numeric,6)                    AS cotizacion,
  ROUND(flm.cost_usd::numeric,2)              AS total_usd_db,
  flm.flags
FROM fact_labor_month flm
JOIN dim_person dp ON dp.id=flm.person_id
WHERE flm.project_id=${PROJECT_ID} AND flm.period_key='${PERIOD}'
ORDER BY persona;
SQL
Chequeos rápidos por persona (deben coincidir con el Excel):
horas_asana / horas_fact (ej: 101.33 / 101.33 para Sol Ayala)
valor_hora_ars (ej: 10270.25)
total_ars_calc ≈ total_ars_db
cost_usd_db ≈ (total_ars_db / cotizacion)
